A Life of Compassion and Love

Celta Camille Head was born in 1958 and lived a life marked by creativity, empathy, and a profound capacity for nurturing others. Though her time on earth was tragically brief, her impact was immeasurable.

Born: 1958

Passed Away: New Year's Eve 1990/91

Location: Athens, Georgia

Known For: Drama Club, Writing, Nurturing Spirit

How They Met

In early 1990, Bruce Whealton met Celta at Georgia Regional Hospital in Augusta, Georgia, where he was volunteering with a social work team after graduating from Georgia Tech. Celta was being treated for anorexia, weighing under 60 pounds when they first met.

"When I met Celta, I explained that I was not approaching her as part of the staff or as part of my role on the social work team. The moment I approached her she smiled before I could even explain these facts." — Bruce Whealton, Three Times A Victim

Their connection was immediate and profound. Bruce would visit her almost daily before she was discharged, and they would walk the hospital grounds together. Celta began writing diary-like letters to Bruce, sharing her innermost thoughts and observations in remarkable detail.

The Transformative Relationship

What developed between Celta and Bruce was more than friendship—it was a healing connection that changed Bruce's life forever. For someone who had never felt truly seen or valued by his family, Celta's unconditional acceptance was revolutionary.

"In her presence, something ancient in me began to heal—the part that had always wondered if I was invisible to the people who should have loved me most... She listened to me with an intensity that startled me. No one had ever listened to me like that before. Like I mattered. Like my voice wasn't just tolerated—it was wanted." — Bruce Whealton, Tell Me I'm Not Invisible

Special Memories

  • Sitting together on the swing outside the hospital, finding privacy and peace
  • Walking the botanical gardens hand-in-hand
  • Daily phone calls where they would always say "I love you"
  • Celta's detailed diary entries, sharing every observation and thought
  • The synchronicity of mind and thought they developed
  • Her arms around him, both smiling, finding comfort in each other

Who Celta Was

Celta was characterized as nurturing, compassionate, empathetic, artistic, and a talented writer. Despite struggling with her own health challenges and addiction, she gave Bruce the validation and intimate connection he had been missing throughout his life.

Celta Head (left) and Van Epperson practicing their lines for a Drama Club performance. Caption reads: "Drama Club member looks over her wardrobe before her performance. (Above) Celta Head and Van Epperson practice their lines."

"After I was with Celta, I felt like I was ten feet tall... confident... worthwhile, and deserving. My self-esteem was higher than it had ever been in my life." — Bruce Whealton, Memoirs of a Healer

The Tragic Loss

On New Year's Eve 1990/91, tragedy struck. Celta died in a house fire in Athens, Georgia, from smoke inhalation caused by an exposed electrical cord on a television.

Bruce received the devastating phone call on New Year's Day, 1991. At the funeral, he cried more than everyone else combined, his grief visceral and overwhelming. Heartbreakingly, Celta's mother instructed him not to attend the burial, denying him the chance for final closure.

"At the funeral, I cried more than everyone else combined. I didn't care how I looked... At the burial the one person who loved Celta most, who felt a visceral sense of grief above and beyond that felt by the others... that one person would be missing. I would not be there." — Bruce Whealton, Memoirs of a Healer

Her Lasting Legacy

Though Celta lived only 32 years, her impact on Bruce's life was profound and lasting. She showed him what it meant to be loved unconditionally, to be someone's most important person, and to experience emotional intimacy without judgment or shame.

"This year with Celta, being seen through the eyes of love, was like waking up for the first time in my life. I was about to turn 24 in April, and the first 23 years of my life were something like a blur or a dream... Lacking something essential until now." — Bruce Whealton, Tell Me I'm Not Invisible

Celta's love gave Bruce the foundation he needed to pursue his calling as a mental health professional, to overcome his own trauma, and eventually to fall deeply in love again with Lynn Denise Krupey. Without Celta's transformative presence, Bruce's journey toward healing and self-actualization might never have begun.

In Bruce's Words

"Despite the tragic loss of Celta, I gained valuable personal growth. She taught me that I was worthy of love, that I could be seen and valued, and that connection with another human being could heal the deepest wounds. Her memory lives on in every person I've helped as a therapist, in every word I've written about the power of love and attachment, and in my heart forever."
