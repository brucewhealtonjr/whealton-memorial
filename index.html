A Memorial to Love, Courage, and Lasting Impact

This memorial honors two extraordinary women whose lives, though cut short, left an indelible mark on the heart and soul of Bruce Whealton and all who knew them.

Through love, they taught the meaning of being seen, valued, and cherished. Through courage, they faced life's challenges with grace and determination. Through their memory, they continue to inspire healing, hope, and human connection.

Celta Camille Head

1958 - 1991

"She Made Me Feel Seen"

"In her presence, something ancient in me began to heal—the part that had always wondered if I was invisible to the people who should have loved me most... She made no demands, offered no judgments, and for the first time, I felt seen." — Bruce Whealton, Tell Me I'm Not Invisible

Celta was Bruce's first deep love, a nurturing soul who offered unconditional acceptance during his early twenties. Their relationship, though brief, was transformative—teaching him that he was worthy of love and capable of profound connection.

Lynn Denise Krupey

1967 - 2015

"The Love of My Life"

"Do not EVER doubt that I was not totally and completely in love with Lynn. That is something that can be known to be true above all else! There are few things in life that I know or believe for certain. My love for Lynn is one of those things that I know with absolute certainty." — Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Lynn was Bruce's life partner, fiancée, and greatest love. A poet, artist, and woman of remarkable courage living with Cystic Fibrosis, she shared over a decade of deep partnership with Bruce—creating a home, a poetry magazine, and memories that would last forever.

From Bruce's Heart

Both Celta and Lynn gave me something I had never known in my childhood: the feeling of being someone's most important person. They listened, they cared, they believed in me when I struggled to believe in myself.

After Celta's tragic death in 1991, I thought I might never love again. Then Lynn entered my life in 1992, and I discovered that love could be even deeper, more passionate, and more transformative than I ever imagined.

These memorial pages honor their lives, preserve their stories, and ensure that their impact on this world—and on me—is never forgotten.

With eternal love and gratitude,
Bruce Martin Whealton
