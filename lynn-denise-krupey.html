Lynn Denise Krupey - Beloved poet, partner, and brave soul

A Love Story For the Ages

Lynn Denise Krupey was born in 1967 and lived a life of creativity, courage, and profound love. A poet, artist, and woman of remarkable strength living with Cystic Fibrosis, she shared over a decade of deep partnership with Bruce Whealton, creating a home, a poetry magazine, and memories that would last forever.

Born: 1967

Passed Away: 2015 (from cancer)

Hometown: Wilmington, North Carolina (Wrightsville Beach)

Education: English degree

Passions: Poetry, Pottery, Creative Writing

Together: 1992 - 2000s (engaged 1994)

The Beginning: Poetry & Destiny

April 1992 - The First Sighting

Bruce had just moved to Wilmington, North Carolina, and started attending weekly poetry readings at the Coastline Convention Center. Among all the attendees, one woman stood out: Lynn Denise Krupey.

"I had just moved to Wilmington in April and I wanted to get to know the people there. So, I started attending poetry reading sessions... I would see Lynn every Sunday at the poetry readings... For me, she stood out among all the attendees that were present there." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Memoirs of a Healer

July 4, 1992 - The First Date

After three months of gathering courage, Bruce finally asked Lynn to join him for a special poetry reading in Carolina Beach on the Fourth of July. What followed was a day that would change both their lives forever.

"So, now, it was July 4th of 1992. I picked her up at her home on Wrightsville Beach. We drove through Wilmington and continued toward Carolina Beach. It was somehow amazing just how easy the conversation was going for both of us." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

They walked out to the jetty together. Bruce reached for Lynn's hand‚Äîand she took it.

"I reached out my hand to her. 'Wow!' I thought, 'She took my hand. Wow!'... My fingers crossed over her palm between the thumb and first finger on her hand. I felt a tingling sensation beginning in my fingers and rising up my arm, like the small soft waves beside us. The sensation came to rest in the center of my chest." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

October 1992 - The First Kiss

After work at Corning, they came to Wrightsville Beach and sat near Johnnie Mercer's Pier. The moment was perfect‚Äîno hesitation, just pure synchronicity.

"There was the synchronicity of desires... I moved toward her instinctually and without hesitation or fear.... Her head was tilted slightly back and turned a bit to the right as my face tilted to the right. Her blond hair waved a bit in the gentle wind. I reached my arm over onto the sand, then brought my lips to hers." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

July 2, 1993 - "I Love You"

Lynn took the initiative to define their relationship, asking Bruce if they were more than friends. His answer would seal their forever.

"Lynn said, 'Are we more than friends... do you want to be more? Do you want to be boyfriend and girlfriend?' ... I said, 'Yes, definitely.' ... I said, 'I am so glad you asked this. It's important. You are so important to me. ... I love you,' I said without thinking and her eyes lit up like something amazing. She answered, 'I love you too.'" ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Late May 1994 - The Engagement

At a jewelry store in the mall, Bruce gave Lynn an engagement ring. What followed was the happiest moment of his entire life.

"They measured Lynn's finger... She placed the ring on her finger, tears running down her face. Then she brought her arms around my neck and brought her lips to mine. My legs started to get weak. ... Making Lynn this happy was a memory more intense than anything since. I have never been happier. She was crying tears of joy and this was bliss!" ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Life Together

Home on Brucemont Drive

Lynn and Bruce created a home together north of Wilmington on Brucemont Drive‚Äîa beautiful coincidence in the street name. There they built a life filled with love, creativity, and mutual support.

Word Salad Poetry Magazine

Together, they co-founded and published Word Salad Poetry Magazine, sharing poetry from local and international poets. The magazine ran from 1995 through the early 2000s, a testament to their shared creative vision.

A Partnership of Equals

"What I am hinting at is the fact that unlike the notions I got from family and my other observations from our culture, men don't have to take the lead. We don't and shouldn't make the first move, expecting our wives and girlfriends to agree to what is happening... There was not even a hint of role expectations." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Public Love

"When we were together, everything about us said that there was no one else in our lives. Two creative types falling in love know what they feel... A touch, a look, a smile, was a declaration of our love. We were two poets sharing our love publicly like reciting a poem." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Living with Cystic Fibrosis

Lynn was born with Cystic Fibrosis (CF), a rare genetic disease that affects about 30,000 people in the US. CF causes excess mucus to build up in the lungs and digestive tract, leading to scarring and decreased lung capacity over time.

"It's also important to note that the girl of my dreams, the love of my life, the one person I would fall madly and passionately, totally and completely, in love with, had a chronic illness called Cystic Fibrosis." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Daily Management

Lynn had to take multiple pills with every meal and use inhalers and medications to maintain her health. Bruce learned the tapping exercises to help loosen mucus in her lungs. They drove together over two hours to her clinic appointments in Chapel Hill, where doctors monitored her oxygen saturation and lung capacity.

The Insurance Dilemma

Despite being engaged and living as husband and wife, Lynn and Bruce couldn't legally marry. To qualify for the state health care plan that covered her CF treatment, Lynn's income had to remain below a certain threshold. As a married couple, their combined income would have exceeded that limit, potentially ending her medical coverage.

"As I mentioned, Lynn and I couldn't have a wedding because our combined income might make her ineligible for the state health plan that would cover her treatment... The fact that we didn't have a wedding didn't change anything. So, my commitment to Lynn was forever." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Her Courage

Despite the challenges, Lynn lived with remarkable grace and determination. She didn't let CF define her‚Äîshe was a poet, an artist, a partner, and a force of nature who refused to be diminished by her illness.

The End of an Era

In 2000, as Lynn's health declined, their life together faced its greatest challenge. Lynn eventually returned to her mother's home on Wrightsville Beach for additional care and support. Though they didn't officially break up, Lynn told Bruce she might not come back. There was never closure‚Äîjust a painful, unresolved parting.

"On about the fourth of September of 2000, I heard Lynn tell me that she might not come back to me. I couldn't even begin to have a 'logical' conversation about this because I broke down and started crying." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Bruce tried to reconnect with Lynn in the years that followed, but it never worked out. Lynn died in 2015 from cancer. Bruce discovered this loss long after it happened, adding another layer of grief‚Äîhe was not mentioned in her obituary.

"Some people have questions like what happened to my first wife, Lynn. She died in 2015, I found out. From cancer. There had been no 'we' for all these years. Merely talking about her and what happened has been so painful." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Memoirs of a Healer

Eternal Love

"During that next decade, I was still in love with Lynn. I would break down in tears ten years after we went on a different path... There was no closure. I had failed to just say those words. I love you! And with that, I will end this book...." ‚Äî Bruce Whealton, Overcoming Shyness & Loving Lynn

Lynn's Legacy

Lynn Denise Krupey lived courageously, loved deeply, and left an indelible mark on everyone who knew her. Her poetry, her strength in facing chronic illness, her playful spirit, and her capacity for unconditional love transformed Bruce's life and gave him the foundation to help countless others as a therapist and writer.

She is remembered not just for the years they shared, but for the love that never died‚Äîa love that continues to inspire and heal, even now.

Rest in peace, Lynn. You are loved. You are remembered. You are eternal.
